Goal Architecture
Macro & Micro-Conversions — What actions do we want users to take?
MACRO-CONVERSION (PRIMARY GOAL)
Book a free site visit / consultation call. A single
booked consultation can be worth Rs.20L–Rs.2Cr
in contract value. This is the only action that directly
generates revenue.
WHY 'BOOK A CALL' BEATS 'CONTACT US'
In Chennai's construction market, trust is
transactional. Clients do not buy online — they buy
after meeting someone. The CTA must lower the
barrier to that first human interaction, not send a
generic form into a void.
Micro-Conversions (Trust-Building Stepping Stones)
• Use the construction cost calculator — highest-engagement micro-action. User inputs plot size, gets instant
Rs./sqft estimate. Captures intent signal early.
• Browse the project gallery (filter by type: villa / apartment / commercial) — visual trust builder. 2+ minutes in
gallery = hot lead.
• Watch a 30–60 second project walkthrough video — dramatically increases trust vs. static photos.
• Download 'Complete guide to building your home in Chennai' PDF — captures phone/email, turns anonymous
visitor into named lead.
• Click the WhatsApp chat button — Chennai users heavily prefer WhatsApp. A 'Chat now' button on every page
converts impulsive curiosity into real conversations.

TOP ENTRY POINTS (HOW USERS ARRIVE)
1. Google Search (organic) — 'construction

— 'home builders Chennai,' 'villa construction

and before/after videos drive direct traffic 4.

INNER PAGE SELF-SUFFICIENCY RULE
Every page must be able to convert a cold visitor
without requiring navigation to the homepage. Each
page must contain: a clear company explanation (2
sentences), one primary CTA ('Book a free call'), at
least one trust signal (logo, project count, or
testimonial), and a WhatsApp button.
Note: Maximum Clicks to Conversion Rule: The contact/booking form must be reachable in 1 click from anywhere on the
site. Sticky header with 'Book a call' CTA button visible at all times. On mobile: a fixed bottom bar with 'WhatsApp us' +
'Book a call' always visible.

Competitive Landscape & Strategic Positioning
What the competition gets wrong, and how to own that gap
Competitor Weaknesses in the Chennai Market
WEAKNESS 1 — NO PRICING TRANSPARENCY
Almost every Chennai construction website hides
pricing. They force users to call and sit through a
sales call just to get a number. This creates
massive friction and loses 60–70% of leads who
won't call.
WEAKNESS 2 — GENERIC COPY & STOCK PHOTOS
Most sites use identical language ('quality
construction, timely delivery') and stock images of
construction workers who look nothing like Chennai.
Zero personality, zero differentiation.
WEAKNESS 3 — TERRIBLE MOBILE UX
WEAKNESS 4 — NO CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE
Most Chennai construction sites are slow (8–14
second load times on 4G), not mobile-responsive,
with tiny contact buttons and no WhatsApp
integration. A mobile-first site alone is a competitive
advantage.
Competitor sites are digital brochures, not
conversion engines. No CTAs above the fold, no
cost calculator, no lead magnet, no chat. Visitors
arrive, read nothing useful, and leave.

Technical Performance — The Hidden UX Layer
Speed and infrastructure requirements for Chennai's market
TARGET FCP
Less than 1.8 seconds. First
Contentful Paint on 4G mobile.
Non-negotiable for Chennai's
Android-first user base.
TARGET LCP
TARGET CLS
Less than 2.5 seconds. Largest
Contentful Paint (hero image).
Core Web Vitals requirement for
Google ranking.
Less than 0.1. Layout stability
score. Ensures content does not
jump around as the page loads.
HOSTING & INFRASTRUCTURE
Deploy on Vercel or Cloudflare Pages (edge CDN).
Serve assets from CDN node in Mumbai/Singapore.
Use Next.js or Astro for static site generation. This
ensures fast load times across all Chennai
locations.
MEDIA OPTIMISATION (CRITICAL — PROJECT PHOTOS
ARE HEAVY)
Convert all images to WebP format. Lazy-load all
images below the fold. Hero image: maximum
150KB, preloaded. Use responsive srcset for
mobile/desktop. Project gallery photos are the
biggest performance killer on construction sites.
MOBILE-FIRST DOM REQUIREMENTS
Thumb-friendly tap targets (minimum 48x48px). No
horizontal scroll. Font size minimum 16px body.
Fixed bottom bar (WhatsApp + Book Call) visible at
all times. Avoid heavy JavaScript libraries that block
rendering.
WHY SPEED MATTERS SPECIFICALLY IN CHENNAI
A significant portion of Chennai construction
prospects browse on budget Android phones
(Redmi, Realme) on Airtel/BSNL 4G. A site that
loads in 3+ seconds will lose a prospect before they
read the first word. Speed is the first trust signal.

Success Metrics & Continuous Iteration
KPIs — What to measure from day one and how to improve it
LEADING KPIS (EARLY SIGNALS — MEASURE WEEKLY)
Cost calculator usage rate · Project gallery scroll
depth · WhatsApp button click rate · Scroll depth on
homepage (target: 60%+ reach 'Packages') · Time
on site (target: 2:30+ minutes) · Mobile bounce rate
(target: below 55%)
LAGGING KPIS (FINAL OUTCOMES — MEASURE
MONTHLY)
Number of consultation calls booked per month ·
Consultation-to-signed-contract rate (target:
20–35%) · WhatsApp conversations that convert to
site visits · PDF download lead conversion rate ·
Google Ads cost per booked consultation
TOOLS TO SET UP ON LAUNCH DAY
Google Analytics 4 (events: calculator_used,
cta_clicked, whatsapp_clicked) · Hotjar (heatmaps
+ session recordings) · Google Search Console ·
WhatsApp Business API
FIRST 90-DAY ITERATION PLAN
Days 1–30: Establish baseline. Watch Hotjar
recordings daily. Identify where users drop off. Days
31–60: A/B test the hero headline (2 variants). Test
CTA button colour/text. Days 61–90: Optimise the
pricing page based on scroll data. Add FAQ section
based on WhatsApp questions received.
